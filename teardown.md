# Primary Growth Brief

**The moment**

Primary just won Emerging Fintech of the Year, has 40+ customers, and a direct competitor (Bracket) raised $7M in February 2026. The category is being validated — the question is whether Primary builds a repeatable GTM machine before Bracket does. That window is now.

---

**The ICP to win first**

**AU/NZ Series A–C companies that raised in the last 90 days.**

Why this segment, why now:
- A funding announcement = $5–50M just hit a bank account earning near-zero yield. That's Primary's exact moment.
- These companies are operationally stretched post-raise — treasury is the last thing on their mind, which is why nobody's called them yet.
- Primary's best existing wins (Lyka, Neara, Nexl) all cluster around funding events. This isn't random — it's the signal.
- The trigger is public. It's on LinkedIn, TechCrunch, Crunchbase. There's no reason to rely on referrals when the moment is this visible.

---

**Three bets**

**1. Funding-trigger outbound engine** *(build first)*

The insight: The week a company raises is the highest-intent window for treasury. Most companies get 15 LinkedIn congratulations messages and zero calls about what to do with the cash.

Execution: Monitor AU/NZ tech funding announcements (Crunchbase, TechCrunch, LinkedIn). Score each company against Primary's ICP. Fire personalized outbound to CFO/Head of Finance within 48 hours of announcement. One person running this systematically could generate 5–10 qualified demos per week from signal alone.

*See the Prospect Scout tool in this repo — it automates the research, scoring, and first-draft outreach.*

Success metric: <48h time-to-outreach after funding announcement; 15%+ reply rate on triggered sequences.

---

**2. VC partnership program** *(highest leverage per effort)*

The insight: VCs see their portfolio companies raise before Primary does. A warm intro from a VC partner is worth 10 cold emails — and VCs are highly motivated to help their portfolio extend runway (which Primary literally does).

Execution: Build a "Primary for Portfolio" program with Blackbird, Square Peg, and AirTree. Three firms × ~70 portfolio companies each = 200+ pre-qualified warm intros. Offer a co-branded "Series A Treasury Checklist," a portfolio audit session, and a referral dashboard so VCs can see the impact they're driving.

The pitch to VCs is simple: you helped your portfolio raise — now help them not waste it.

Success metric: 3 VC partnerships signed in Q1; 1+ new customer per partner per quarter.

---

**3. "Going Global" content flywheel** *(builds defensible moat)*

The insight: Primary's real differentiation vs. Mercury and Trovata is multi-entity, multi-currency, international. But this isn't fully reflected in the content. "The treasury layer for growth companies" is broad — the sharper frame is "the platform for AU companies going global."

Execution: Build a "CFO's Guide to Going Global" content hub targeting the moment a company opens a US LLC or UK entity. Anchor it in Eucalyptus, Lyka, and Nexl case studies. Distribute via Ben's LinkedIn + targeted paid. This creates inbound at exactly the right point in a company's journey — when they just got complex enough to need Primary.

Success metric: 500 monthly organic visits; 10% demo request conversion.
